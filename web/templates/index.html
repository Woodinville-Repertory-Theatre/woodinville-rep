Larissa FastHorse is the first indigenous American woman to have a play on Broadway. Join us for a thought-provoking evening as we present satire that “flips the bird” on the original thanksgiving meal. Set in the world of a Thanksgiving pageant, this witty comedy explores cultural stereotypes, political correctness, and the complexities of history with sharp humor and insightful commentary.

This production promises an engaging and entertaining experience that will leave you reflecting on the true spirit of Thanksgiving. Don’t miss your chance to be part of this compelling theatrical event!

Set in the early 1900s, “Silent Sky” by Lauren Gunderson tells the remarkable true story of Henrietta Leavitt, a brilliant astronomer. Working at the Harvard Observatory, Henrietta’s passion for the stars drives her to make groundbreaking discoveries about the universe. As she maps the distant galaxies beyond our own, she faces the challenges of her time, including societal norms that restrict her place in science. Experience the wonder and inspiration of Henrietta Leavitt’s story in “Silent Sky,” a beautiful and thought-provoking play that highlights the brilliance of a woman whose work changed the world.
